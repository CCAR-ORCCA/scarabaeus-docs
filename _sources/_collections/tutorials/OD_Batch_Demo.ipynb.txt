    "## 2. Dynamics Models Catalogue\n",
    "\n",
    "Scarabaeus provides a **modular force model system** through `ForceModelTranslation`.\n",
    "You turn on each perturbation with keyword flags.  This section documents every available option.\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "### 2.1 Point Mass Gravity (Keplerian)\n",
    "The simplest model: the spacecraft is attracted only by the central body (Sun in our case).\n",
    "No keyword needed — this is always included.\n",
    "\n",
    "```python\n",
    "fm = scb.ForceModelTranslation(primary_body=sc)\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**When to use:** quick propagation checks, very short arcs, or when perturbations are negligible.\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "### 2.2 Third-Body Gravity\n",
    "Gravitational pull from other solar-system bodies (planets, Moon, etc.).\n",
    "Pass a list of SPICE body names.\n",
    "\n",
    "```python\n",
    "fm = scb.ForceModelTranslation(primary_body=sc,\n",
    "                               third_bodies=['EARTH', 'JUPITER BARYCENTER', ...])\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**Effect:** at 1 AU from the Sun, Earth and Jupiter perturbations are on the order of 10⁻⁶ km/s²\n",
    "over a 3-day arc — significant for precision OD.\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "### 2.3 Cannonball Solar Radiation Pressure (SRP)\n",
    "Isotropic SRP model.  Uses `area` and `ref_coeff` from the Spacecraft object.\n",
    "The SRP scale factor η_SRP can be estimated as a static parameter in the OD state.\n",
    "\n",
    "```python\n",
    "fm = scb.ForceModelTranslation(primary_body=sc, cannonball_SRP=True)\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**When to use:** missions without attitude data, or as a first-order SRP estimate.\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "### 2.4 N-Plate SRP\n",
    "Attitude-dependent SRP computed face-by-face from a plate configuration file.\n",

    "fm = scb.ForceModelTranslation(primary_body=sc, nplate_SRP=True)\n",

    "## 3. Dynamics Tuning\n",
    "\n",
    "**Dynamics tuning** means choosing which force models to include and verifying their fidelity\n",
    "against an independent truth (SPICE ephemerides or higher-fidelity propagation).\n",
    "\n",
    "### Workflow\n",
    "1. Propagate with model A (low fidelity) and model B (high fidelity).\n",
    "2. Compute the position difference over the arc.\n",
    "3. If the difference exceeds your OD accuracy goal → model A is insufficient.\n",
    "4. If the difference is negligible → model A is acceptable (and cheaper).\n",
    "\n",
    "### Rules of thumb for a heliocentric 3-day arc at 1 AU\n",
    "| Force | Magnitude [km/s²] | Position effect over 3 days |\n",
    "|-------|-------------------|-----------------------------|\n",
    "| Sun PM gravity | ~6×10⁻³ | dominant |\n",
    "| Earth 3-body | ~6×10⁻⁷ | ~0.2 km |\n",
    "| Jupiter 3-body | ~2×10⁻⁸ | ~0.01 km |\n",
    "| Cannonball SRP | ~5×10⁻¹⁰ | ~0.001 km |\n",
    "\n",
    "For sub-kilometre OD accuracy, Earth and Venus third-body gravity is **essential**.\n",
    "SRP becomes important for light spacecraft over long arcs.\n",
    "\n",
    "### Process noise tuning\n",
    "When using sequential filters (next notebook), the process noise covariance **Q** must be tuned:\n",
    "- Too large Q → filter accepts noise, inflated covariance.\n",
    "- Too small Q → filter diverges when unmodelled forces accumulate.\n",
    "\n",
    "A starting heuristic: set Q to (expected unmodelled acceleration)² × dt.\n"
   ]

    "    state_def = scb.StateDefinition.from_components([\n",

    "## 6. Reference Trajectory (Perturbed Initial Conditions)\n",
    "\n",
    "In real OD we do not know the true initial state; we start from a **reference trajectory**\n",
    "obtained from a previous solution or orbit determination arc, then apply the filter to correct it.\n",
    "\n",
    "Here we simulate this by **perturbing** the true initial state:\n",
    "- Position: ±1 km error (typical for deep-space missions after several days without tracking)\n",
    "- Velocity: ±1 mm/s error\n",
    "\n",
    "The filter's job is to recover the deviation `δx = x_true − x_ref` at each measurement epoch.\n",
    "\n",
    "Each filter iteration uses a **new Spacecraft object** (unique SPICE ID) so that its SPK kernel\n",
    "does not overwrite the truth or previous iteration's kernel.\n",
    "\n",
    "### Covariance initialisation\n",
    "The **initial covariance matrix P₀** encodes our uncertainty in the reference initial state.\n",
    "Rule of thumb: set the 1-σ values to roughly the size of the perturbation.\n"
   ]
  },
  {
   "cell_type": "code",
   "execution_count": null,
   "id": "21",
   "metadata": {},
   "outputs": [],
   "source": [

    "delta_vel_kms = np.array([1e-3, 1e-3, 1e-3])    # 1 mm/s velocity error in each axis\n",
    "\n",
    "delta_pos = scb.ArrayWUnits(delta_pos_km,  km)\n",
    "delta_vel = scb.ArrayWUnits(delta_vel_kms, km/sec)\n",
    "\n",
    "pos_ref = scb.ArrayWFrame(pos_0.quantity + delta_pos, frame)\n",
    "vel_ref = scb.ArrayWFrame(vel_0.quantity + delta_vel, frame)\n",
    "\n",
    "# ── reference spacecraft (iteration 1) ───────────────────────────\n",
    "Orbiter_ref = scb.Spacecraft('Orbiter_Ref_it1', -1001,\n",
    "                              dry_mass+fuel_mass, area, cr)\n",
    "\n",
    "state_ref = scb.StateDefinition.from_components([\n",
    "    ('position', 3, 'estimated', 'dynamic', Orbiter_ref, pos_ref),\n",
    "    ('velocity', 3, 'estimated', 'dynamic', Orbiter_ref, vel_ref),\n",
    "])\n",
    "sv_ref = scb.StateArray(epoch=epoch_0, origin=origin, state=state_ref)\n",
    "\n",
    "# ── propagate reference with same force model as truth ───────────\n",
    "fm_ref = scb.ForceModelTranslation(\n",

    "## 7. Batch Filter: Least-Squares Batch (LSB)\n",
    "\n",
    "### Theory\n",
    "The Least-Squares Batch (LSB) filter solves the normal equations\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\\mathbf{x}^* = \\left(\\mathbf{H}^T \\mathbf{W} \\mathbf{H} + \\mathbf{P}_0^{-1}\\right)^{-1}\n",
    "  \\mathbf{H}^T \\mathbf{W} \\mathbf{y}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "where **H** is the measurement sensitivity matrix, **W = R⁻¹** is the measurement weight matrix,\n",
    "**y** are the pre-fit residuals, and **P₀** is the initial state covariance (acts as a Tikhonov\n",
    "regulariser).\n",
    "\n",
    "The solution is iterated: after each batch solve the reference trajectory is updated and the\n",
    "process repeats until `‖δx‖ < convergence_threshold`.\n",
    "\n",
    "### Measurement specification\n",
    "`MeasurementSpec.many()` or `MeasurementSpec.from_dict()` combine multiple measurement datasets\n",
    "into the single structure the filter expects.\n",
    "\n",
    "### FilterSettings\n",
    "`FilterSettings` gathers all configuration:\n",
    "- `initial_covariance` — P₀\n",
    "- `process_noise` — `ProcessNoiseSettings` (batch filters: usually not used; sequential: SNC/DMC)\n",
    "- `output` — `OutputSettings` for controlling what is saved\n"
   ]

    "## 8. Batch Filter: SRIFB + Static Parameter Estimation (η_SRP)\n",
    "\n",
    "### Theory\n",
    "The **SRIF Batch (SRIFB)** is the numerically superior square-root formulation of the batch filter.\n",
    "Instead of accumulating the normal matrix **H^T W H** (which can become ill-conditioned), it works\n",
    "with the square root of the information matrix **R̄** via QR decomposition.\n",
    "\n",
    "### Static Parameter Estimation\n",
    "The OD state can include **static scalar parameters** that do not propagate through dynamics but\n",
    "affect the measurements through their force model coupling.\n",
    "\n",
    "A common example is the **SRP scale factor η_SRP**: if our cannonball model is imperfect\n",
    "(wrong area or reflectivity), η_SRP absorbs the mismatch.\n",
    "\n",
    "```\n",
    "F_SRP_corrected = η_SRP × F_SRP_nominal\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "The filter simultaneously estimates η_SRP alongside the 6-state trajectory.\n",
    "\n",
    "### Estimation vs. Considered Parameters\n",
    "- **`estimation='estimated'`** — parameter is fully solved for; its covariance shrinks with data.\n",
    "- **`estimation='considered'`** — parameter is *not* solved for, but its uncertainty propagates\n",
    "  into the state covariance (conservative covariance inflation without biasing the solve-for state).\n"
   ]
  },
  {
   "cell_type": "code",
   "execution_count": null,

    "## 9. Stochastic Acceleration: Piecewise Gauss-Markov (PFOGM)\n",
    "\n",
    "### Theory\n",
    "When unmodelled non-gravitational accelerations exist (outgassing, solar wind fluctuations,\n",
    "attitude-dependent SRP mismodelling), we can augment the state with **stochastic acceleration\n",
    "parameters** that are solved for alongside the trajectory.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Piecewise FOGM (PFOGM)** divides the tracking arc into $N_b$ batches of length $\\Delta T$.\n",
    "Within each batch, the acceleration is assumed constant (zero-correlation between batches).\n",
    "This gives $3 N_b$ additional scalar parameters in the state vector.\n",
    "\n",
    "### When to use PFOGM vs. SNC\n",
    "- **PFOGM (batch)**: Use when you want to *estimate* the unmodelled acceleration in each time\n",
    "  interval as a deterministic parameter. Residuals should show flat, white-noise character after.\n",
    "- **SNC (sequential)**: Use when you want the filter to *absorb* process noise in real time\n",
    "  without adding to the state dimension. More appropriate for sequential estimators (next notebook).\n",
    "\n",
    "### Parameter naming convention\n",
    "- `a_fogm`  → 3-component first-order Gauss-Markov acceleration (continuous FOGM)\n",
    "- `a_pfogm` → 3×N_batches piecewise-constant acceleration vector\n"
   ]

    "## 10. Measurement Editing\n",
    "\n",
    "**Measurement editing** (also called *data editing* or *outlier rejection*) removes corrupted or\n",
    "anomalous observations before they degrade the OD solution.\n",
    "\n",
    "Scarabaeus supports three editing strategies configurable via `FilterSettings`:\n",
    "\n",
    "### 10.1 Chi-Squared Filter (automatic, recommended)\n",
    "For each observation, compute the normalised residual\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\\chi_i^2 = \\frac{(y_i - \\hat{y}_i)^2}{\\sigma_i^2}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "If $\\chi_i > \\sigma_\\text{scale}$ (default 3), the measurement is flagged as an outlier.\n",
    "This is the standard 3-σ editing used in operational OD.\n",
    "\n",
    "```python\n",
    "settings = scb.FilterSettings(\n",
    "    initial_covariance = cov,\n",
    "    editing_method     = 'chi_squared',\n",
    "    editing_kwargs     = {'sigma_scale': 3.0},\n",
    "    ...\n",
    ")\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "### 10.2 Date-Range Filter (manual)\n",
    "Remove all measurements outside a specified UTC time window:\n",
    "\n",
    "```python\n",
    "settings = scb.FilterSettings(\n",
    "    editing_method = 'date_range',\n",
    "    editing_kwargs = {'start': t_start_et, 'end': t_end_et},\n",
    "    ...\n",
    ")\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "### 10.3 Lasso Selector (interactive)\n",
    "Opens a matplotlib figure where the analyst can draw a lasso to select outliers:\n",
    "\n",
    "```python\n",
    "settings = scb.FilterSettings(\n",
    "    editing_method = 'lasso',\n",
    "    editing_kwargs = {'sigma_scale': 3.0, 'preview': True},\n",
    "    ...\n",
    ")\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "Best practice: run 1–2 iterations without editing to converge to a good reference,\n",
    "then apply chi-squared editing and re-run.\n"
