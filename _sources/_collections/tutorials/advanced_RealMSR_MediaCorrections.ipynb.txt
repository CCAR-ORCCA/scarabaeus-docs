    "## 2.0 — Tropospheric Delay Models\n",
    "\n",
    "Radio signals traveling between a DSN ground station and a deep-space spacecraft must pass through Earth's troposphere. The neutral atmosphere introduces a **non-dispersive path-length delay** that is independent of signal frequency and must be modeled and subtracted from the raw observations before they can be used for orbit determination.\n",
    "\n",
    "SCB decomposes the zenith tropospheric delay into two additive components stored in separate JSON correction files:\n",
    "\n",
    "| Component | File | Model function |\n",
    "|---|---|---|\n",
    "| **Seasonal** | `1972_001_2048_001_tro_modified.json` | `MediaCorrections.mediafun_trig` — trigonometric annual + semi-annual fit |\n",
    "| **Non-seasonal** | `2010_001_2010_032_tro.json` | `MediaCorrections.mediafun_nrmpow` — normalized power-law fit |\n",
    "\n",
    "Both files contain a nested dictionary keyed by site (`C10`, `C40`, `C60`) and wet/dry component (`WET`, `DRY`). The static method `MediaCorrections.get_media_data_for_et()` retrieves the coefficient sub-dictionary that brackets a given epoch."

    "## 2.3 — Elevation mapping functions\n",
    "\n",
    "The zenith delays computed above apply only when the signal travels straight up. For any real ground-station-to-spacecraft link, the signal travels at a slant angle, increasing the path length through the atmosphere. The **mapping function** `m(E)` converts the zenith delay `δ_z` to a slant delay `δ_s`:\n",
    "\n",
    "```\n",
    "δ_s = m(E) × δ_z\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "SCB implements the **Chao (1972) mapping function** via `MediaCorrections.chao_mapping(elevation_rad)`, which returns separate dry and wet mapping factors. Both approach 1.0 at zenith (90°) and grow rapidly toward the horizon where the signal path through the atmosphere is longest.\n",
    "\n",
    "> **Note:** Measurements at very low elevation angles (below ~10°) are typically excluded in OD processing because the mapping function uncertainty becomes large and multipath effects increase."

    "## 3.0 — Ramp Table Manager\n",
    "\n",
    "DSN uplink signals are not always transmitted at a constant frequency. When a ramp is in effect, the exciter steps through a pre-programmed frequency-vs-time schedule stored in the tracking data as a **ramp table** (SFDU record type 9). Each ramp table entry records:\n",
    "\n",
    "| Field | Description |\n",
    "|---|---|\n",
    "| `sec` | Second-of-day at which the ramp interval begins |\n",
    "| `doy` | Day-of-year of the ramp interval |\n",
    "| `ramp_freq` | Exciter frequency at the start of the interval [Hz] |\n",
    "| `ramp_rate` | Linear frequency rate during the interval [Hz/s] |\n",
    "| `ramp_type` | Integer flag identifying the ramp mode |\n",
    "\n",
    "The `scb.RampTableManager.integrate()` method integrates the piecewise-linear frequency profile between two epochs `(t1, t3)` and returns the total accumulated phase, which is needed to correctly compute Doppler and sequential ranging observables when the uplink is ramped."
   ]

    "## 3.2 — Integrated frequency sensitivity\n",
    "\n",
    "The integrated uplink frequency between times `t1` and `t3` is needed to convert a sequential ranging observable (in Range Units) to a time-of-flight.     "\n",

    "\n",

    "---\n",
    "## 4.0 — RTLT Media Corrections for Sequential Ranging\n",
    "\n",
    "In the previous section, we computed the **zenith** tropospheric delay — the path-length error for a signal traveling straight up through the atmosphere. When applying corrections to real measurements, we need the **round-trip light-time (RTLT)** delay, which accounts for both the uplink leg (ground → spacecraft, at transmit time `t1`) and the downlink leg (spacecraft → ground, at receive time `t3`).\n",
    "\n",
    "The workflow is:\n",
    "\n",
    "1. Load a tracking data file and identify the ground station\n",
    "2. Extract the receive epochs `t3` from the sequential ranging observable\n",
    "3. Compute the transmit epochs `t1` using a quick RTLT estimate from SPICE\n",
    "4. Evaluate `media_correction.computed_rtlt_delays()` at both `t1` and `t3`\n",
    "5. Add the two one-way delays to get the total RTLT media correction\n",
    "6. Convert from meters → seconds (via the speed of light) → Range Units (via the uplink carrier frequency)\n",
    "\n",
    "This corrected delay is subtracted from the raw sequential ranging observable before it is used in the OD filter."
   ]
  },

    "## 4.2 — Compute transmit epochs t1\n",
    "\n",
    "The sequential ranging observable is tagged at the **receive** time `t3`. The transmit time `t1` (when the uplink tone left the ground station) must be estimated for the uplink media correction. A two-step SPICE light-time computation gives a good approximation:\n",
    "\n",
    "1. Compute the **downlink** one-way light time at `t3` — the time for the signal to travel from the spacecraft to the ground station.\n",
    "2. Subtract the downlink light time from `t3` to get the approximate spacecraft time, then compute the **uplink** one-way light time back to the station.\n",
    "3. The RTLT is the sum of the two, and `t1 = t3 − RTLT`."
   ]
  },

   "execution_count": null,

    "target = \"-64\"   # OSIRIS-REx NAIF ID\n",
    "frame  = ITRF93\n",
    "\n",
    "# Downlink one-way light time: spacecraft → ground at t3\n",
    "dl_rtlt = scb.SpiceManager.get_lighttime(\n",
    "    trgt_bdy=target,\n",
    "    epoch_time=SRA_t3.times.values,\n",
    "    reference_frame=frame.name,\n",
    "    obsvr_bdy=GS1.name,\n",
    "    aberration_correction=\"CN+S\",\n",
    ")\n",
    "\n",
    "# Uplink one-way light time: ground → spacecraft (at the epoch when signal arrived)\n",
    "ul_rtlt = scb.SpiceManager.get_lighttime(\n",
    "    trgt_bdy=GS1.name,\n",
    "    epoch_time=SRA_t3.times.values - dl_rtlt.values,\n",
    "    reference_frame=frame.name,\n",
    "    obsvr_bdy=target,\n",
    "    aberration_correction=\"CN+S\",\n",

    "## 4.4 — Compute RTLT tropospheric delays\n",
    "\n",
    "`media_correction.computed_rtlt_delays()` evaluates the full tropospheric model (seasonal + non-seasonal, dry + wet, with Chao mapping applied) at a given ephemeris time and returns the one-way path-length correction in **meters**.\n",
    "\n",
    "The RTLT correction is the sum of the corrections at `t3` (downlink) and `t1` (uplink):\n",
    "\n",
    "```\n",
    "Δp [m]   = Δp(t3) + Δp(t1)\n",
    "Δτ [s]   = Δp [m] × 10⁻³ / c          (Moyer eq. 10-27, converting km → m → s)\n",
    "ΔΦ [RU]  = Δτ [s] × f_uplink [Hz]\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "where `c` is the speed of light in km/s from `scb.constants.c`."
   ]
  },

   "id": "25",

    "SRA_tropo_t1 = []\n",
    "\n",
    "for ii in range(len(SRA_t3.times.values)):\n",
    "    t3 = SRA_t3.times.values[ii]\n",
    "    t1 = SRA_t1_epoch.times.values[ii]\n",
    "\n",
    "    p_t3 = media_correction.computed_rtlt_delays(query_time_et=t3, target_spice_id=target)\n",
    "    p_t1 = media_correction.computed_rtlt_delays(query_time_et=t1, target_spice_id=target)\n",
    "\n",
    "    SRA_tropo_t3.append(p_t3)\n",
    "    SRA_tropo_t1.append(p_t1)\n",

    "SRA_tropo_t1 = np.array(SRA_tropo_t1)\n",
